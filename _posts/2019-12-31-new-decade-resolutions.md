## Procrastinate less
### Why
Procrastination is a terrible weight on me. It damages my grades, annoys my friends, and makes working on a long-term personal project nearly impossible. I want to stop procrastinating now more than I ever have in my life.

### How
There are two main things I plan on changing to curb procrastination. First, I will schedule my day out in high detail. Work and homework will come first, then personal projects, then free time. Taking Jordan Peterson's advice, I will make a schedule that I actually want to follow, not one that is torture. Secondly, I will try to do my productive tasks in a different environment than where I spend my free time. My campus has plenty of study spaces and outdoor areas with tables. I want to take advantage of these to limit my distractions.

## Spend less money
### Why
I spend too much money on meaningless things. I've put more money into my Steam library than my car cost, yet barely played the majority of those games. I'm constantly ordering things on Amazon, only to toss that item in a drawer and forget about it a week later. This isn't a big deal right now because I don't make much money and therefore can't waste too much money, but once I'm out of college and making a decent paycheck, this could be seriously destructive. I need to change my habits now before I seriously harm my finances.

### How
One major thing I need to do is create a budget. I used to have one but stopped when I went to college. I should save a decent amount of my income and only allow myself to spend a small amount of it. One idea to curb impulse buys is to wait 24-hours before purchasing anything. However this could prove inconvenient for certain purchases, so I doubt I could apply this to all online purchases.

## Exercise
### Why
Exercising not only makes you more physically fit, but also improves mental health: tiredness, anxiety, focus, willpower, etc.

### How
I need to schedule my exercise into a time that is convenient and unlikely to be interrupted. Early in the morning is a good time for this, but it's hard to find motivation in the morning to get dressed and walk to the gym. I need to think more about how to get consistent exercise in.

## Eat healthier
### Why
Eating healthier not only will assist in muscle growth, but also improves mental health.

### How
I want to significantly decrease my sugar intake. I don't think it would be possible for me to entirely avoid sugar, but I can definitely stop drinking soda, eating candy, etc. Sugar-free alternatives can be used to satisfy any cravings. Other than sugar I just need to make sure that I'm eating balanced, healthy meals and eating a salad at least 1-2 times a week.

## Have a consistent sleep schedule
### Why
An inconsistent sleep schedule messes with your circadian rhythm, which leads to tiredness, lack of focus, etc. Additionally, a consistent sleep schedule makes it much easier to build habits.

### How
I'm not yet decided on my sleep schedule, but it will probably be something like this: Go to bed at 11 pm, wake up at 6 am. This allows me to (hopefully) exercise in the morning before work or class, and still have enough time in the afternoon to have some fun after working on homework and personal projects.

## Read more books
### Why
I used to read a lot of books. I would skim non-fiction books constantly and read a youth novel in 1-2 days. This all stopped when I got a tablet that had access to YouTube. Now I watch YouTube while eating meals instead of reading. Now I watch YouTube before bed instead of reading. Etc. I believe this has ruined my attention span. I have a huge backlog of books that I want to read, but just never do because YouTube is much more fun.

### How
One major thing is to block social media apps on my phone, mainly Reddit and YouTube. My phone allows me to block apps, but still take timed breaks where I can access them. I want to only read one book at a time, otherwise its too hard to ever finish a book. My main goal is to just read one page a day of a book. This isn't much, but I will likely read more anyway.
